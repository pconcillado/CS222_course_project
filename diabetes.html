What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic health condition that affects how your body uses blood sugar (glucose), in other words, how your body converts food into energy. It is characterized by consistent hyperglycemia episodes (high blood sugar) during fasting periods. Hyperglycemia can lead to serious health problems, including kidney disease and hypertension.

    Types of Diabetes:
  1. Type 1 diabetes
    • This is caused by an autoimmune reaction where the body attacks insulin producing cells in the pancreas.
  2. Type 2 diabetes
    • This occurs when the body becomes resistant to insulin or doesn't produce enough.
  3. Gestational Diabetse
    • This is caused by hormonal changes during pregnancy that affects insulin.

    Common symptoms:
  1. Frequent urination
  2. Excessive Thirst
  3. Extreme hunger
  4. Unexplained weight loss
  5. Fatigue and Irritability
  6. Blurred vision
